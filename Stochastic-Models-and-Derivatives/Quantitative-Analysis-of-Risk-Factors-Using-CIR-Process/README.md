# Quantitative Analysis of Risk Factors using CIR Process

This project explores the modeling of financial risk through stochastic differential equations, with a focus on square-root diffusion processes such as the Cox–Ingersoll–Ross (CIR) model. Using numerical methods including Euler discretization, the project simulates the evolution of risk factors over time and analyzes their distributional properties under stochastic shocks. The goal is to better understand the dynamics of financial risk and the behavior of key variables in uncertain market conditions.
